# Examples

These examples show the decision style. They are not claims about the books' exact wording.

## Example 1: Crypto Airdrop Strategy

User:

我想投入很多钱包刷一个潜在空投，值不值得？

Taleb-style diagnosis:

- Tail type: fat-tail leaning. Airdrops are highly skewed, policy-driven, and winner-take-most.
- Randomness vs skill: some process skill exists, but final allocation rules are opaque.
- Ruin risk: not financial ruin if sizing is small, but wallet安全、时间成本、合规和钓鱼风险可能出局。
- Payoff: convex only if downside is capped. If资金、私钥、主钱包或时间被无限拖进去，就从 convex 变成 fragile。
- Skin in the game: 项目方、脚本作者、KOL、工作室和你承担的风险不对称。

Recommendation:

Proceed only as a barbell:

- Protect core assets: separate wallets, no main wallet signing, strict approval hygiene.
- Cap cost: fixed budget and fixed time window.
- Create many small independent attempts, not one large exposed wallet.
- Avoid leverage, borrowed funds, and irreversible KYC/legal exposure.
- Exit if the rule set becomes increasingly opaque or requires unsafe signatures.

## Example 2: Leaving A Stable Job For A Startup

User:

我要不要辞职全职做一个项目？

Taleb-style diagnosis:

- Tail type: startup returns are fat-tailed.
- Main issue: do not compare average salary to imagined upside. Compare ruin risk and optionality.
- Fragility: quitting too early may convert a convex experiment into a fragile all-in bet.
- Antifragile path: keep base protected while running real market experiments.

Better structure:

- Define runway and family obligations as ruin constraints.
- Run customer discovery, paid pilots, or distribution tests before quitting.
- Quit only when the project has evidence, not just narrative.
- Preserve upside through equity/control/IP while limiting personal downside.
- Use a staged threshold: nights/weekends -> part-time -> full-time.

Verdict:

Usually `Proceed as small experiment` before `Proceed all-in`, unless the current job itself creates irreversible harm or the startup opportunity is time-sensitive with capped personal downside.

## Example 3: Buying A High-Return Financial Product

User:

这个理财产品年化很高，历史上没亏过，可以买吗？

Taleb-style diagnosis:

- The phrase "历史上没亏过" is not safety. It may indicate hidden short-volatility exposure.
- Smooth return history can be a red flag if the underlying exposure is opaque.
- Ask whether the product collects small gains while carrying rare catastrophic losses.

Checklist:

- Is the strategy transparent?
- Is there leverage?
- Who holds the assets?
- What happens under liquidity stress?
- Is redemption guaranteed?
- Are losses marked daily or hidden?
- Did similar products die and disappear from the sample?
- Does the seller share downside?

Verdict:

`Avoid` or `tiny capped allocation only` unless the user can identify the underlying exposure, legal claim, custody, liquidity, and worst-case loss.

## Example 4: Product Launch

User:

我们要不要花三个月做一个大版本再发布？

Taleb-style diagnosis:

- Product-market fit is opaque and feedback-rich, so large unreleased builds are fragile.
- The plan depends on prediction instead of real feedback.
- Antifragile redesign is to expose small versions to reality quickly.

Better structure:

- Release a narrow prototype.
- Measure real behavior rather than stated preference.
- Keep architecture modular so failed assumptions are cheap.
- Preserve optionality in positioning, pricing, and feature depth.
- Avoid commitments that require one grand launch to be correct.

Verdict:

`Redesign first`: reduce batch size and create learning loops.

## Example 5: Expert Recommendation

User:

专家都建议这么做，我是不是该听？

Taleb-style diagnosis:

- Credentials are not enough in opaque domains.
- Ask whether the expert pays for being wrong.
- Prefer track records with visible downside exposure and survival across regimes.

Questions:

- Did they make this call before the outcome?
- What did they lose when wrong?
- Are they rewarded for certainty, complexity, or actual results?
- Do they use the same recommendation with their own money, reputation, or operations?

Verdict:

Take expert advice seriously when it comes with transparent assumptions, error history, and skin in the game. Discount confident advice that transfers downside to you.
