## High-value Patterns To Adopt

### 1. Follow-Up Beats One-Off Calls

He repeatedly revisits earlier posts: TLM, LAB, VELVET, MYX, BAS, XPIN, SYN, SXL, H/SAHARA-style cases. This is valuable because the edge often appears after the first anomaly:

- first abnormal transfer,
- then CEX path,
- then OI/funding change,
- then price reaction,
- then follow-up transfer.

System rule: every P0/P1 alert should keep a follow-up clock. If no confirmation arrives, downgrade confidence. If confirmation arrives, escalate with the new evidence.

### 2. CEX Path Interpretation Is Central

Recurring pattern:

- project or associated wallet sends tokens to Bitget/Gate/KuCoin/Bybit/Kraken/ASTER;
- CEX cold wallet sends to hot wallet;
- hot/deposit wallet sends to fresh wallets;
- fresh wallets later sell or wait.

Adopted rule:

- Exchange inflow from project/MM/early-holder paths is sell pressure.
- Exchange outflow to fresh wallets is possible accumulation or inventory staging.
- Cold-to-hot transfer needs next-hop classification before action.
- Unknown wallet after CEX movement requires a watch state, not a final verdict.

### 3. Combine Chain With OI/Funding

He often pairs chain movement with:

- OI size,
- funding rate,
- long/short pressure,
- FDV and MC.

Examples from reviewed posts:

- LAB: chain sells plus rising OI/funding stress.
- VELVET: wash/flow plus OI expansion after earlier manipulation.
- MYX: exchange cycling plus OI relative to MC.
- BAS/XPIN: on-chain anomaly plus current OI state.

Adopted rule: no directional conclusion from wallet flow alone when derivatives dominate. Require `flow + OI/funding + price reaction` for stronger action.

### 4. Distinguish Token Archetypes

He treats "regular army" old coins differently from low-cap manipulated tokens:

- old/larger coins move on news, business cooperation, tech upgrades, sector rotation, and broad market;
- low-cap/Alpha/meme/manipulated tokens need wallet, CEX, holder, OI, and microstructure.

Adopted rule: assign token archetype first. Use different evidence weights by archetype.

### 5. Sector Liquidity Migration Matters

He explicitly notices when meme tokens absorb liquidity and secondary "妖币" opportunities thin out.

Adopted rule: daily strategy needs a sector-flow note. If liquidity migrates to meme, reduce confidence in weak secondary setups unless fresh CEX/chain evidence appears.

### 6. Listing and Deposit Windows Are Trading Events

He tracks Upbit/Bithumb/Binance/Bitget announcements and deposit reopenings.

Adopted rule: for listings and deposit changes, capture:

- announcement URL,
- deposit open time,
- trading start time,
- pair,
- whether deposit status changed before price move,
- related exchange wallet movement.

### 7. Early Signal Requires Patience

He often warns that early chain activity is left-side discovery and entry timing needs broader data.

Adopted rule: `early anomaly` creates watchlist priority; entry waits for confirmation. The system should say "watch" when only the first leg exists.

### 8. Do Not Ignore Recycled Manipulation

VELVET/MYX-style posts show that prior manipulation does not end the opportunity. Projects can re-accumulate, wash, reset OI, and restart.

Adopted rule: archive cases as dormant, not dead. Continue low-frequency monitoring after distribution if the project still has liquidity, CEX access, and OI capacity.

### 9. Time-of-Day Risk Exists

H-style posts highlight overnight transfer and sell events.

Adopted rule: major unlock, known distribution, or high-risk wallet events need time-based monitoring. If the user is holding through an event window, define an overnight risk plan.

## What To Use With Caution

- His posts are fast and practical, but many are inference-heavy. Treat them as discovery and case study, then verify.
- Screenshots and Arkham links need local chain reconstruction before becoming system truth.
- Some posts include hype or social engagement mechanics. Exclude those from the strategy layer.
